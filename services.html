From the moment a finding lands to the day it’s sustainably closed — every step is structured, evidence-producing, auditable, and owned.

01

Regulatory Trigger

Intake and scope regulatory findings. Establish accountability, risk classification, and remediation timelines before work begins.

Key Activities

  • Analyze and categorize regulatory findings, including MRA/MRIA language, consent order provisions, and audit observations
  • Align findings with applicable regulatory and control frameworks
  • Facilitate definition of 1LOD accountability and 2LOD oversight responsibilities
  • Develop initial risk severity ratings and target remediation timeframes
  • Establish a centralized findings register to organize and track remediation work

Outputs

  • Centralized Findings Register with risk classifications
  • 1LOD / 2LOD accountability reference matrix
  • Regulatory framework cross-reference mapping
  • Target remediation timeline and milestone roadmap
  • Baseline structure for board and executive reporting
“The Board needs confidence that every regulatory finding has an owner, a deadline, and a credible plan—before a single control is touched.”
02

Diagnose

Root cause analysis, control gap mapping, and risk-based prioritization. Understand what actually broke before designing a fix.

Key Activities

  • Structured root cause analysis using established frameworks (e.g., 5 Whys, fishbone, control failure analysis)
  • Control gap mapping aligned to items in the findings register
  • Distinction between preventive and detective control gaps as part of the analysis
  • Risk-informed prioritization to help sequence remediation by exposure and impact
  • Identification and documentation of potential systemic vs. isolated failure patterns

Outputs

  • Root Cause Analysis (RCA) summaries aligned to relevant findings
  • Control Gap Assessment report
  • Risk-informed remediation backlog
  • Control gap inventory
  • Observations on potential systemic patterns and contributing factors
“You cannot fix a regulatory problem until you understand exactly how the control failed.”
03

Design

Control redesign and uplift, evidence standards definition, KCI/KRI design. Define controls to operate, not just to document.

Key Activities

  • Control redesign and uplift addressing identified root causes and control gaps
  • Evidence standard definition — clarifying what constitutes credible proof of control operation
  • KCI (Key Control Indicator) and KRI (Key Risk Indicator) design, including indicative thresholds
  • Alignment of control structure with GRC traceability (linking requirements, risks, and controls)
  • Design of a practical 1LOD / 2LOD operating model for the revised control environment

Outputs

  • Control blueprint documentation (preventive and detective control patterns) aligned to findings
  • Evidence standards matrix describing expected artifacts and supporting documentation
  • KCI/KRI definitions with indicative thresholds and monitoring cadence
  • Traceability mapping across requirements, risks, and controls (for GRC integration)
  • Example testing and validation approaches to support control monitoring
“A control that cannot produce evidence of its own operation cannot withstand regulatory review.”
04

Deliver

Iterative remediation, evidence production, and issue closure packages. Progress is delivered through controlled increments, supported by evidence—not narratives or slide decks.

Key Activities

  • Structured remediation work cycles aligned to prioritized findings and remediation milestones
  • Evidence artifact development, including policy updates, configuration documentation, validation testing results, and training records
  • Periodic working reviews with first-line control owners and second-line compliance oversight functions
  • Issue closure package preparation, assembling supporting documentation and validation materials for supervisory review
  • Ongoing executive and Board-level reporting on remediation progress, milestones, and emerging risks

Outputs

  • Evidence artifact packages produced in each remediation cycle
  • Issue Closure Packages for individual findings, organized for supervisory or regulatory review
  • Executive and Board-level remediation progress dashboards
  • First- and second-line review and acknowledgement documentation
  • Remediation timeline tracking, including progress against planned milestones
“A finding closes only when the evidence can stand on its own in the examination file.”
05

Sustain

BAU ownership transition, automated evidence collection, KCI/KRI monitoring. Built to prevent the next finding before it's written.

Key Activities

  • BAU ownership transition — structured handoff from remediation workstream to designated operational control owners
  • Automated evidence collection enablement (where applicable) to support repeatable control verification
  • KCI/KRI monitoring dashboard configuration and threshold alignment with risk appetite
  • 1LOD self-assessment and 2LOD oversight model alignment to support ongoing control validation
  • Repeat-finding prevention review and historical look-back analysis to identify structural weaknesses

Outputs

  • BAU Transition Playbook by control domain
  • KCI/KRI monitoring dashboards for ongoing control visibility
  • Automated evidence collection runbooks supporting repeatable evidence generation
  • 1LOD / 2LOD operating procedures for post-remediation control oversight
  • 12-month sustainability validation roadmap
“The goal isn’t closing a finding — it’s building controls that prove, every day, the problem stays solved.”
